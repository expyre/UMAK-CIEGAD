ACCOMODATION SERVICES

We provide free access to higher education for persons with disabilities. Students who are formally diagnosed or assessed as having learning difficulties that limit their capacity to learn must meet the standard university admission requirements, with the following additional processes:

1. Endorsement and Academic Records

Applicants must be endorsed by their former school.
They must submit an academic performance report, including details of interests, strengths, limitations, and an Individualized Educational Plan (IEP), if available.

2. Medical Assessment

Submit a medical assessment or evaluation report, if available.

3. Center for Profiling Interview

The applicant and their parent(s) or guardian(s) must undergo an interview with the center for profiling.

4. Program Identification Interview

After completing the regular admission processes, the applicant will have an additional interview with the Dean of the receiving college to determine the most suitable program

Curriculum Modification

Learn More

CURRICULUM MODIFICATION

Submitted academic performance reports and profiling records from the center will be thoroughly evaluated. In coordination with the receiving colleges, the curriculum and course content will be tailored to focus on skills development, emphasizing entry-level skills and competencies required by our industry partners.
Key points include:

1. Industry-Driven Instruction

Course design prioritizes practical skills aligned with industry demands.

2. Adaptive Learning

The teaching approach will be adjusted based on classroom interaction and student needs.

3. Collaborative Support

Academic Coaches (Professors) and the center will collaborate to determine if partial inclusion strategies are necessary to enhance learning outcomes.

Programs

PROGRAMS

Program placement for students follows the university's admission and retention policies. As the Center for Inclusive Education and General Academic Development (CIEGAD) and inclusive education initiatives are still in their early stages, students are initially admitted into associate programs.
Key considerations:

1. Associate Program Entry

Students begin with associate programs as a foundational step.

2. Performance-Based Advancement

Advancement to baccalaureate degree programs is contingent on students' performance in the associate programs.

3. Student Choice

Students who demonstrate the required competencies may qualify to pursue baccalaureate degrees based on their preferences.

Coaching

Learn More

COACHING SERVICES

The coaching program is a collaborative effort involving:
Peer Coaches (students)
Academic Coaches (professors)
Job Coaches (volunteers and industry partners)
Life Coaches (staff from the center)

This service aims to support students in overcoming challenges related to:

1. Academic Performance

Assistance with understanding instructions and submitting projects.

2. Skill Development

Guidance on teamwork, social interactions, and behavioral/emotional management.

3. Personal Matters

Addressing issues that students openly share.

To ensure the effectiveness of coaching, training and orientation sessions are provided, focusing on:
Specialized strategies, such as sign language training.
Tailored interventions to enhance the teaching-learning process.
Activities that promote inclusivity and engagement.

Coaching sessions are designed to be intermittent, encouraging students to develop independence and achieve self-actualization. ×

On-The-Job Training (OJT)

Learn More

DIRECTED IMMERSION

Directed Immersion involves a collaborative effort among the training center, the college, and industry partners, beginning one semester before the scheduled on-the-job training. Job Coaches identify and outline the specific skills and competencies required for students to ensure their readiness and successful integration into the workplace.

This phase is designed to familiarize students with their future work environment while simultaneously preparing industries to address accessibility needs. Weekly meetings are held between students, center representatives, and Job Coaches to help students feel comfortable in their future training sites and to proactively address any potential concerns prior to deployment.

The team of coaches collaborates to refine course content with a strong focus on skills development and mastery, aiming for the ultimate goal of future employment for the students.

GUIDED ON-THE-JOB TRAINING

Guided OJT involves close supervision and support from a designated OJT professor or coordinator, who regularly accompanies students to observe their adaptation to the workplace. Each visit includes the preparation of anecdotal reports that document student performance in areas such as skills acquisition, teamwork, socialization, adaptability, emotional and behavioral adjustments, functionality, and independence.

A scaffolding approach is employed to build students' self-esteem, self-reliance, and trust in their Job Coaches and industry personnel. Regular consultations with all stakeholders, including parents, are conducted to facilitate follow-up sessions at school and reinforce skills at home through face-to-face consultations.
